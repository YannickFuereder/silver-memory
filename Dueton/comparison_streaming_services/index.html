Spotify is (besides YouTube Music) the most used sercive on our list. It has this large success thanks to the broad availability of popular songs and podcasts. On the other hand there are for sure many people which just use spotify because there's a free version with (in contrast) very few ads and limitations.

This service offers a completely free, but limited, version which is financed by ads every 30 minutes. You can also test the premium version for 3 months. After that you can either switch back to the free version or start paying 4,99€ a month. Or you could team up with up to 6 friends and subscribe to the family plan which is 14.99€/month (around 2.50€ per Person).

Another quite interesting feature of spotify is Spotify Connect. It allows you to seemlessly stream your music to another device where you've installed spotify on or even stereos with builtin support without even touching either of them.

Apple Music is a fairly new service (it's availabe since 2015) but, mostly thanks to it's integration into the apple ecosystem gained many users. Abos for Apple's music streaming service are placed pretty similar to spotify (more on that later) which makes it pretty interesting for apple users altough there are much less playlists on this platform.

Apple Music also offers a free premium subscription for 3 months. After this period of time you'll need to either pay the 9.99€ a month or cancel the service. Alternatevely you could also subscribe to the family plan (14.99€) and team up with up to 6 friends (altough you technically have to live at the same place as them). Another option is to pay for Apple One which comes with a couple of Apple premium services.

Altough there's no real alternative to Spotify's Connect builtin in Apple Music all devices in their Ecosystem do support Airplay, which pretty much does the same as Spotify Connect, but isn't cross-platform.
